Information

Is it really worth it to get a GED?
Getting a GED can open a world of job and educational opportunities to its holder. Many employers require that prospective employees have a GED to demonstrate they have a basic level of education to start at many entry level positions . Furthermore, a GED recipient can go on to vocational school or college.
How do I get one?
In the greater the metro atlanta there are a number of education centers that provide GED classes. There are also some online providers of the GED, however, ensure that if you chose to take it online that it is an accredited online program. Furthermore, the GED is an exam that can ONLY be taken in person
What are costs associated with the GED exam?
The four tests are on: Reasoning through Language Arts (Literacy, 2 hours, 30 minutes with a 10 minute break); Mathematical Reasoning (Math, 1 hour, 55 minutes); Science (1 hour, 30 minutes); and Social Studies (1 hour, 10 minutes), and the price tag is $40 per test section ($160 for the entire battery of four tests). You may take one test at a time, and scores are valid for two years from your first registration.
How do I register to take the GED?
Go to the computer access section
What if I fail a subject?
In Georgia, there is no waiting period for your first two retakes. After taking a test subject 3 times, you'll have to wait 60 days before retesting. There's no limit to how many times you can test in a year. You will receive two discounted retakes for every GED® test subject you purchase but don’t pass. GED Testing Service waives its $20 testing fee, however your state may require a separate fee. In Georgia, your first two retakes will be $20 if the retakes occur within 12 calendar months. Any additional retakes will be $40.
