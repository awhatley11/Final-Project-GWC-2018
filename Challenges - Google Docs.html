Challenges 
 
 
●Learning a New Language:​ ​In order to create the database we had 
to use a coding language called SQL, which none of us had ever used 
before. Therefore, some group members had to take it upon themselves to 
learn SQL to create our database 
 
●Finishing Everything: ​There was so much information and features 
we wanted to add to the website, however, all of it was not realistically 
manageable to do within a week. So, working within the timeframe and 
having all the necessary code done before the code freeze was difficult as 
well.  
  
Monetization 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Our website is a non-profit organization, 
therefore we would like our work to be used 
by all members of the community. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
Future Work 
 
In the future, we would like to further augment our work, by creating a more 
expansive job database, and also by including scholarships for further 
education, to make it. Also, another feature we would have liked to add was 
the inventory calculator, which could be used to more easily track incoming 
and outgoing donations.  
 
Acknowledgements: 
 
This presentation would not have been possible without Keisha James, the Girls 
Who Code Atlanta executive sponsor, or Vanessa White and Sarah Raheja, 
the two co-site leads, who set up this amazing experience. Furthermore, we 
would like to Meg, Caroline, Matt, and Victoria for setting up this amazing 
classroom and going above and beyond to answer the myriad of questions we 
had. And lastly, we would like to give a shout out to Sarah Raheja (again) 
and Mary Wade, our mentors, for guiding us through the idea process and 
assisting us with our pitch. None of this would have been possible without any 
of you all!  
 
 
 
 
  
Solutions 
 
 
 
 
81% of the United States population has access to 
the internet and often times this service is paid 
for before other basic utilities such as water or 
gas. Thus, we figured this would be an excellent 
platform to assist with the resolution of both 
permanent and transitional homelessness as well 
as unemployment and education barriers.  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Using the power of the internet we hope 
to connect people with job opportunities, 
how to videos, and information on 
further education. There one can find a 
myriad of career paths based upon a 
certain criteria. One can also find how 
to videos and informative resources on 
other life skills, such, as financial 
literacy, so that he or she can construct a 
stable foundation for his or her future.
